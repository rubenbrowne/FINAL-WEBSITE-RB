Carp Posters



The carp task is based on the annual hot air balloon festival in Bristol, Europes largest festival of its kind...

In my first hot air balloon poster, I imagined the title having a soft indigo like colour with hot air balloons surrounding it. I started with my background, adding a light blue hazey gradient to signify the sky, following by the bristol suspension bridge; I knew I had to include the bridge within my posters at is it a key landmark of the city. I finally settled on a pastel blue colour theme, adding a few hot air balloons witih low opacity. Lastly I added a strip of colour with all of the contributing organisations, giving a streamline and subtle look.












In my second hot air balloon poster, I wanted to add a little bit more contrast for an exciting look and also wanted to incorporate some ab-normal shapes. I added a 'blob' like shape to the middle of the poster, adding in the suspension bridge, as I loved the feeling of being in the festival itself! I also warped the text and added white outliines shadowing the key infomation which turned out great in my opinion. Adding in the strip of the organisations and lastly adding a dottedt overlay to tie in the whole poster.

Typography


The typography task is based on showing and designing the 8 typography systems


Transitional


An organised but fluctuating typography system, I ustalized waves to show the fluidity of transitional.



Radial


Text in a radial format reads from a specific radius, it can be represented clearly when coming off a circle.



Axial


The axial format is used frequently in posters and presentations, its commonly disorientating way of showing text is great for creative projects.



Bilateral


Bilateral, like axial, creates text off a specific axis, however bilatertal text only revolves around one axis, coming from the side, middle and top.



Modular


The modular system is very similar to the grid format, however unlike the grid, the text does not follow a pattern (x and y axis).



Dilational


One of my favourite systems, the dilational format shows text expand outwards and revolve round a point in a circular fasion.



Grid


The classic text format, used for formal and infomational texts often, the clear and precise gird format divides the space into even sections.



Random


As it states, the random format has no specific pattern or spacing, just random text dispersed on a page.
